A Hunter's Dream

Deer Hunting

When I was younger, the only thing I wanted to do was deer hunt. I used to watch old VHS tapes for hours of people taping their hunts. These hunters would kill monster bucks which just taunted me because I was creating a passion for an activity I had never even done. About a year or two goes by and my father finally took me on my first deer hunt. At that time, I was the most determined 5-year-old in the state of Georgia to kill a deer. I found out real fast that the gun he gave me to kill my first deer did not even fire.

As time went on, my passion for deer hunting disappeared and got replaced with duck hunting. My best conclusion as to why is that I got tired of not getting the opportunity to kill a big buck. Last year that streak of no opportunity ended when I was bow hunting. The biggest buck I had ever seen in person stepped out in front of me within 25 yards, an easy bow kill. Sadly, I shot and missed. I was sick to my stomach, but looking back now that was one of the greatest things to happen to me. Missing that once in a lifetime shot also relit a fire in my soul. The passion and eagerness to deer hunt were back at last but more than ever. I was ready for redemption, and I knew I would not miss my shot again.

This year, thanks to my father, I got my shot at redemption and did not accept failure as an option. A deer we named Flop stepped out almost 200 yards in front of me the first time I hunted my new stand. This time I was rifle hunting. I patiently waited for Flop to turn broadside so I could get a clean shot at him. While I waited, I tried my best to gather my breath and calm my nerves. When I saw my opportune shot, I exhaled softly and squeezed the trigger. The only thing I saw after that was my dream/goal ever since a 5-year-old finally accomplished. Flop, a true monster in the state of Georgia, scored 153 inches and was the biggest buck anyone in my family has ever killed in Georgia.
